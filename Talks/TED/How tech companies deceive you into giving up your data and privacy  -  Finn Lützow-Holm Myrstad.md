# Summary of How tech companies deceive you into giving up your data and privacy | Finn Lützow-Holm Myrstad

The video discusses how tech companies deceive users into giving up their data and privacy by exploiting vulnerabilities in devices that are connected to the internet. The author argues that users should be given more understandable terms before agreeing to them, and that companies need to prioritize privacy and security over other business goals.

This video discusses how tech companies deceive users into giving up their data and privacy by exploiting vulnerabilities in devices that are connected to the internet. The company that made and programmed Cayla was found to have been selling the doll for more than a year after Finn Myrstad and his team published a report exposing the doll's vulnerabilities.

00:05:00
The author of the video discusses how technology companies deceive users into giving up their data and privacy by hiding terms and conditions that are difficult or impossible to understand. They show how reading the terms on an average phone takes longer than watching the "Harry Potter" and "Godfather" movies combined, and how users are unlikely to understand them. The author argues that users should be given more understandable terms before agreeing to them.

00:10:00
In this video, Finn Lützow-Holm Myrstad shares examples of how tech companies deceive users into giving up their data and privacy. He argues that companies need to prioritize privacy and security over other business goals, and governments need to create a safer internet to protect users' rights. He also encourages users to use their voices to advocate for more privacy-respecting technology.
